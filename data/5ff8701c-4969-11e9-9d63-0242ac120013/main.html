Analysis of microstructure and tensile properties produced by cryogenic laser peening on 2024-T351 aluminum alloy

Abstract

The microstructural response and tensile properties on 2024-T351 aluminum alloy specimens induced by cryogenic laser peening were investigated. The microstructure behaviors were characterized by transmission electron microscopic examination and electron backscattered diffraction analysis. Tensile properties tests at room temperature were also performed. The experimental results cleared that compared with room temperature laser peening, cryogenic laser peening can induce higher-density of dislocation and smaller-size grain, while the strength and plasticity of the processed 2024-T351 aluminum alloy were simultaneously increased. The analysis results suggested that the more excellent tensile properties were attributed to the more beneficial microstructural evolution induced by cryogenic laser peening.

Introduction

Due to its advantages, such as high strength to weight ratio, resistance to fatigue, and improved corrosion properties, 2024-T351 aluminum alloy has been widely used in the manufacture of high-load mechanical components in the aerospace field [1,2]. Unfortunately, various failure behaviors, such as frictional wear, corrosion, fatigue and fracture, are very likely to occur in the service process with complex loads, resulting in great threats and injuries to the national economy and personal safety [3,4]. Therefore, it is of great practical significance to further enhance the comprehensive mechanical properties of 2024-T351 aluminum alloy.
As a competitive and effective surface strengthening technique, Laser Peening (LP) can effectively produce the beneficial microstructure and compressive residual stress on the surface layer of treated materials, thereby improving their mechanical properties [5–7]. For example, Maozhong Ge et al. [5] analyzed the effects of LP on microstructure and fatigue crack growth rate of AZ31B magnesium alloy. And their research results suggested that LP could induce high compressive residual stress and nanocrystalline layer, which attributed to the decrease of fatigue crack growth rate. Lei Zheng et al. [6] investigated the performance of micro-dent array fabricated by LP on the surface of A304 stainless steel. The experimental results found that the improvement of wear performance of A304 stainless steel may due to the combined effects of improved surface microhardness and the existence of compressive residual stress induced by LP. In addition, Rujian Sun et al. [7] also found that after LP, high density of dislocations and mechanical twins were generated and resulted in the increase of micro-hardness, a less misorientation grains with weak texture were achieved, as well as residual stresses were modified from tensile to compressive state with a maximum value around 100 MPa. Meanwhile, many studies have also shown that the synergistic effect of cryogenic process and deformation strengthening technique can provide better microstructure and comprehensive mechanical properties for the treated metallic materials [8,9]. Especially, Ye and collaborators [10,11] explored the effects of Cryogenic Laser Peening (CLP) on the microstructure and mechanical properties of a variety of metallic materials. Their research results demonstrated that compared with room temperature laser peening (RT-LP), CLP can induce more beneficial microstructures and play an important role in further heightening the mechanical properties of treated materials.
In the paper, the purpose of our research was to explore the effects of RT-LP and CLP on the microstructure and tensile properties of 2024-T351 aluminum alloy. At the same time, the difference of the microscopic strengthening mechanism for the enhancement of tensile properties between the processes of RT-LP and CLP was also compared.

Experimental procedure

The material used in this study is 2024-T351 aluminum alloy, whose chemical composition is also given in Table 1. The CLP experiments were carried out using a SpitLight 2000 pulsed Nd:YAG solid-state laser with a wavelength of 1064 nm and a pulse width of 8 ns. The spot diameter was 1 mm, and the overlap ratio was 50% as well as the laser energy chosen in this study was 1.6 J. A 120 μm professional aluminum foil was used as the absorbing layer, while a 3 mm thick K9 glass was used as the transparent confining layer. The temperature of the specimens used for CLP treatment was maintained at (−130 ± 2) °C. Electron Backscattered Diffraction (EBSD) analysis was carried out using a Zeiss Sigma 500 Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) system. Meanwhile, the microstructure was characterized by a Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM). An X-ray diffraction with sin2Ψ (X-350 X-ray, produced by Handan stress technologies institute) method was used to measure the Full Width at Half Maximum (FWHM) values on the top surface of the specimens with different treatments. The tensile test at room temperature was performed on a WDW-200G electronic universal testing machine. The load rate selected for this test was 1 mm/min as well as the tensile strain rate was 1.0 × 10 −3 s −1. For each treatment, three specimens were selected for tensile test, and the average values of strength and elongation were taken as the final results. And the dimension and LP area of the specimens used for tensile test at room temperature were presented in Fig. 1. After the tensile tests completed, the fracture was also observed and analyzed using a SEM (Zeiss Sigma 500).

Experimental results and discussion

Fig. 2 presents the EBSD analysis results of the specimens with different treatments. Fig. 2(a–c) displays the orientation imaging microscopy (OIM) maps on selected regions, while red represents [001] orientation, green represents [101] orientation, and blue represents [111] orientation. It is clear that the initial microstructure shown in Fig. 2(a) was consisted of coarse and inhomogeneous equiaxed grains. Fortunately, many refined grains were obtained after LP, while the specimen processed by CLP exhibited the smallest-size grains. It can be concluded from Fig. 2(d–f) that 70.5% of the grains size exceeded 40 μm for the untreated specimen, while only 35.0% and 24.5% of the grain size exceeded 40 μm after RT-LP and CLP. In addition, the average grain size decreased from 40.74 μm to 33.46 μm and 28.30 μm after the processes of RT-LP and CLP, which obtained a decrease of 17.87% and 30.54% compared with the original average grain, respectively. Meanwhile, it is noteworthy from Fig. 2(g–i) that the distribution of grain boundary misorientation angles has changed after LP. For the untreated specimen, 34.5% of the grain boundaries were observed with low misorientation angles (<15°), while more than 65.5% of grain boundaries have high misorientation angles (>15°) ranging from 15° to 60°. However, the percentage of the large-angle grain boundaries obviously decreases and the percentage of the low-angle grain boundaries increases after RT-LP and CLP. After RT-LP, nearly 56% of grain boundaries were found with low misorientation angles, as revealed in Fig. 2(h). In addition, it is quite interesting to found that compared with RT-LP, the percentage of low-angle grain boundaries further increases to 62.5% after CLP.
Meanwhile, the dislocation structures on the top surface of the specimens with different treatments were analyzed by TEM observation, and the dislocation configuration is given in Fig. 3. It can be clearly seen from the comparison between Fig. 3(a) and (b) that CLP can induce higher-density of dislocation structures under the same laser parameters compared with RT-LP. In order to better compare the effect of RT-LP and CLP on the dislocation density of 2024-T351 aluminum alloy, XRD technology was also used to detect surface FWHM values of the samples treated by RT-LP and CLP. It can be noted from Fig. 3(c) that the surface FWHM value of the sample treated by RT-LP was 2.15°, while the value of the sample subjected to CLP was 2.36°, which achieved an improvement of 9.77%. According to the formula proposed by Gay et al., the relationship between FWHM value and dislocation density can be expressed as [12]:
(1)ρ=β2/2ln2πb2
Where ρ is dislocation density, β is FWHM value, b is Burgers vector. It is clear from Eq. (1) that the higher dislocation density corresponds to a higher FWHM value. Therefore, it can be seen from the comparing results of the FWHM values that CLP can provide a higher-density of dislocation structures, which is also consistent with the results shown in Fig. 3(a) and (b).
Additionally, Fig. 4 provides the comparing results of the tensile properties at room temperature of the different treated specimens. Fig. 4(a) is the stress-strain curves at room temperature of the specimens processed by different treatments. Also, it is clear from the comparing results in Fig. 4(b) that the specimen treated by CLP exhibits a highest tensile strength with 506.1 MPa, while those of the untreated sample and the specimen subjected to RT-LP are 423.2 MPa and 462.8 MPa, respectively. As can be seen from Fig. 4(c), the elongation of the untreated sample is 19.27%, while those of the specimens processed by RT-LP and CLP are 19.43% and 20.81%, respectively. Therefore, it is worth noting that the tensile strength and elongation of the specimen treated by CLP is higher than that of the specimen processed by RT-LP, indicating that the process of CLP can significantly raise the strength and plasticity of 2024-T351 aluminum alloy. Additionally, fracture morphologies were also observed so as to further explore the effects of CLP on the tensile properties of 2024-T351 aluminum alloy, as indicated in Fig. 4(d–f). It is clear from Fig. 4(d) that the fracture morphology of the untreated specimen is a typical ductile fracture with some dimples. However, due to the influence of the second phase, impurities and material defects, some quasi-cleavage characteristic was also found locally. Fortunately, after LP, the fracture mode of the specimen did not change significantly, but the dimples on the fracture became larger and the cleaved feature decreased. In particular, CLP process could promote the decomposition and refinement of the second phase and impurities, as well as the bridging of vacancies of the treated materials. Meanwhile, the high-density of dislocation structure and the refined grains induced by CLP simultaneously increase the strength and plasticity of the materials, as well as the tensile specimen breaks after undergoing large plastic deformation, which eventually cause the tensile sample treated by CLP to exhibit a complete dimple fracture and the dimples were also largest and deepest, as demonstrated in Fig. 4(f).
It can be seen from the above comparison results, CLP can induce more beneficial microstructures, which playing a significant contribution to enhancing more excellent tensile properties. On one hand, the volume shrinkage effect in the ultra-low temperature environment gives rise to the generation of compression stress and plastic deformation during the process of CLP, which have a positive effect on improving the plastic flow stress of processed materials [13,14]. On the other hand, due to the decrease of the interatomic distance of the materials in the cryogenic environment, the propagation speed of CLP-induced shock wave is faster, resulting in higher shock pressure, and thus a better effect on the plastic deformation of the treated materials [15]. Due to the proliferation and aggregation of dislocation structures, original grains are divided into sub-crystals, eventually inducing grain refinement [16]. And grain refinement is an effective method to simultaneously increase the strength and plasticity of treated materials. In addition, compared with RT-LP, the ultra-low temperature during the process of CLP also has an important contribution to suppressing the dynamic recovery of proliferated dislocation and refined grain, resulting in higher-density of dislocation and smaller-size grain, thus providing more excellent tensile properties of 2024-T351 aluminum alloy [17].
4

Conclusions

The microstructure and tensile properties of 2024-T351 aluminum alloy subjected to CLP process have been studied. Compared with RT-LP, CLP can significantly induce better effect on grain refinement, and the average grain size decreased from 33.46 μm to 28.30 μm, which decreased by 15.42%. Meanwhile, more low-angle grain boundary was achieved after CLP. Compared with the specimen treated by RT-LP, higher-density of dislocation structure for the CLP specimen was observed, leading to a higher strength and plasticity, while the tensile strength and elongation were increased by 9.36% and 7.10%, respectively.
